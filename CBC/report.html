Report Objectives

Included in this report is:

  • An analysis of outlets and journalists who have recently published messages consistent with the key, positive messaging about topics that may be of interest.
  • An analysis of specific, strategic media frames that are correlated with increased rates of hateful content and may thus pose reputational risk.


Methods

Friendly Outlet Analysis.
We first generated twenty iterations of various positive media messages that would be amenable to your organization using a generative language model. To model amenability, we used the Congressional Black Caucus’s organizational description found on their website. We then used an in-house artificially intelligent search algorithm to find and index example texts that mirrored the sentiment in those twenty positive media messages. All data included in the final report came from our media corpus and were not altered or summarized via LLM, thus posing no risk of hallucinated content.

Reputational Risk Factor: Hate Generating Environment Analysis.
We leveraged a contemporary state of the art hate speech detection model to first identify overtly hateful messages in both news articles and quotes reported in those articles. We then used a word vector model (the guts of contemporary AI) to generate clusters of messages and identified which clusters contained more overtly hateful examples. We then manually performed a deep language analysis (see George Lakoff’s Don’t Think of an Elephant) to assess what conceptual metaphors were being used in the more hateful clusters. All data included in the final report came from our media corpus and were not altered or summarized via LLM, thus posing no risk of hallucinated content.
